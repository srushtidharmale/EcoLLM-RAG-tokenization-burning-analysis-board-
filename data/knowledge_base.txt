Green AI reduces token-level energy by routing simple queries to smaller models and skipping retrieval when unnecessary.

Quantization (4-bit, 8-bit) cuts GPU memory and inference energy for LLMs like Mistral-7B and Qwen2.5 without large accuracy drops.

KV-cache compression and speculative decoding lower per-token compute during long generations.

Adaptive RAG retrieves documents only when the query needs external knowledge; factual lookups from cache skip the vector DB.

Prompt compression summarizes retrieved chunks before sending them to the model, reducing input tokens by 30-60%.

CodeCarbon estimates CPU/GPU energy and CO2 emissions during AI workloads using regional carbon intensity.

Neuromorphic and photonic chips are research alternatives to reduce semiconductor-heavy AI energy demand.

FlashAttention and PagedAttention improve attention efficiency, reducing latency and energy per token on GPUs.

Distillation trains small student models (TinyLlama, Phi-3) to mimic larger teachers for cheaper inference.

Token budget controllers cap max output length to prevent wasteful long generations from chat interfaces.

MoE (Mixture of Experts) activates only subsets of parameters per token, improving efficiency at scale.

Edge AI processors run quantized models locally, avoiding repeated cloud API calls and their energy cost.

Hugging Face and MLCommons publish benchmarks linking model size, tokens, and estimated inference energy.
